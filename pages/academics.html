Academics

Curriculum R2024 with a semiconductor-first path.

The programme moves from engineering foundations to VLSI-oriented analysis, design, verification, and implementation practice.

Download PDF
01

Semester I

Engineering mathematics, applied physics, programming fundamentals, basic electrical and electronics concepts, communication skills.

02

Semester II

Engineering mathematics, network theory, electronic devices, digital systems foundations, problem-solving practice.

03

Semester III

Signals and systems, analog circuits, digital electronics, data structures, semiconductor fundamentals.

04

Semester IV

Microprocessors and microcontrollers, control systems, communication fundamentals, CMOS concepts, laboratory practice.

05

Semester V

VLSI design fundamentals, HDL-based design, embedded systems, digital signal processing, design laboratory.

06

Semester VI

Physical design concepts, verification methods, FPGA-based design, static timing analysis, project-based learning.

07

Semester VII

Advanced VLSI electives, industry-oriented laboratory work, research methodology, major project phase I.

08

Semester VIII

Major project phase II, internship or industry exposure, seminar, and professional readiness.
